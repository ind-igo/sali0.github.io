Goal

Vision

Growth Focused

This technical training program is for creative entrepreneurs who have either an established hobby or a well thought out idea; to grow, they need business training, support, and access to industrial grade technical equipment. Our aim is to grow these into sustainable businesses that increase the number of jobs, grow the income for these owners and employees (grow new employees), and grow the amount of products exported from Tacoma to the world.

Enabling Destiny

Tacoma has always been at the bottom of the big leagues. We have an internationally recognized port, world class museums, are in the nations top 50 for college towns, have a renowned public school system and are home to an international high school. And yet we struggle to retain successful professionals and start-ups. This program has the capacity to grow, nurture, and even attract companies that utilize the industrial nature of Tacoma to make it a realized contender in the big leagues.

As Mayor Strickland pointed out in the State of the City address on February 24th, there are 14 technical incubators in Tacoma. Tacoma residents want this type of program. The existing incubators (and that term is very broad), either focus on providing access to a set of equipment, the support of a co-working space, or business training. We are proposing to create a comprehensive program for businesses to receive all of these in one place. Spaceworks has an excellent track record for its Creative Enterprise Training, and is a natural fit to take that program into a technical, product-centered realm.

Once, we took on the role as the City of Destiny when we were on the frontier of the nation. We can reclaim our heritage by leading at the frontier of space, nanotechnology, robotics, artificial intelligence, communications, and energy!

Mission

Results Oriented

Spaceworks: Technical will create jobs in Tacoma that are different from the kinds of jobs that spin out from the Creative Enterprise program. Spaceworks: Technical builds skills that enable entrepreneurs to make creative products to take to an international market. The results of this program will be measured (through participant businesses) in the number of full time jobs created, total revenues, revenues derived outside Pierce county, and number of patents filed.

Creation Engine

We talk in terms of jobs but what we really mean is employment opportunities self or otherwise. Through this program, we create countless opportunities and help people recognize those opportunities in the skill sets that they have, they gain, and they grow. Our focus is on creative tangible products made with advanced industrial technology in Tacoma, and the benefits these successful companies bring to Tacoma.

Values

Technical Innovation

Fostering a creative spirit with industrial technology leads to important innovations and breakthroughs. Providing business training to guide entrepreneurs through this environment is key.

Incubate Capabilities

The entrepreneurs we are focused on already have a certain skill set. They either: 1) know the tech and have an idea that they dont know how to turn into a business, 2) they know an industry and see a need for a product but dont have the technical skills or business savvy needed, or 3) some combination thereof. Spaceworks: Technical vets their idea and capabilities, and fills in the gaps.

Expand Possibilities

There are three elements to expanding possibilities: community teamwork, tangible financials, and industrial equipment. Like Spaceworks already does with its Creative Enterprise Training Program, Spaceworks: Technical connects entrepreneurs with the social support and educational resources they need to make their business successful. There are economic incentives to participating in a Spaceworks entrepreneurial program through access to content experts and reduced rent in available properties. Finally, access to specific equipment, and the concomitant training to use this machinery forms the third leg of this program.

Motivation and Wish List

Not Altruistic

This business training is a catalytic tool for a bigger agenda. This program exposes people to skills sets needed to work in the commercial space industry, robotics, artificial intelligence, nanotechnology, bionics, supercapacitors, battery tech, life support, hydroponics/vertical farming and a host of other advanced integrative technologies. It is focused specifically on the areas LiftPort Group needs developed to realize the Space Elevator. Because of that larger goal, it will act as a magnet to draw students and incubated companies from around the world.

Satellites + Robots

The main industrial equipment this program will train entrepreneurs on are used to build and research satellites and robotics. These are both industries that are growing rapidly, and the opportunities for spin-off technology and products are infinite. Southern California is where you go to build satellites. To build robots, you head to Detroit, Boston, or Tokyo. There is nowhere in the world to go if you want to integrate these two nascent technologies until now. We ask that the City of Destiny live up to its moniker.

 

Scope

What

Maker-Centric

Spaceworks attracts a wide variety of creative businesses already, and this program would add the capability to address tech product creatives. There is a certain ethos inherent in the maker community: DIYers that are self-reliant and autodidactic; they were often troublemakers in school. They should thrive in this environment. We will adapt the Spaceworks training model accordingly, by stressing practical solutions, providing a structure of available options, guiding (while acknowledging a range of choices) and embracing a think : build : fail : iterate culture.

Training Classes

Spaceworks: Technical is a clone of Spaceworks existing Creative Enterprise Training Program, with a focus on businesses with products in BioTech / Energy / Materials Science / Communications / Computing / Robotics / and Space industries. In addition to training in the business essentials, building a business plan, and pitching their business, participants will gain experience on industrial grade tech equipment, such as additive and subtractive manufacturing (3D printing & laser cutting), electronics, CAD/CAM/CNC, robotics, and nanotech.

How

Now & Later

The current business training through Spaceworks encompasses 8+ sessions, or approximately 24-33 hours of training, over 16 weeks. This expanded program involves 60 hours of training: 30 on the business side and 30 on the technical equipment side, over 13 weeks. The business side of Spaceworks: Technical is exactly what Spaceworks has already. The technical side of Spaceworks: Technical involves how-to and safety courses, prototype development support, and assistance in getting products to market.

With an ideal timeline, the alpha program could be launched in Autumn 2016. In 2017 and beyond, we would like to host 2-3 programs a year, each with 8-15 businesses. With the funding we are asking for here, we could run for two years and six programs. Assume six programs nurture 48-90 companies served.

Using the jobs-creation target metric of $30,000/job, if just 10 of these generate 1 FTE, the program is a success. ($135,000 annually * 2 years = $270,000. $270,000 / $30,000 = 9 for a breakeven. 9 + 1 = 10 therefore a modestly successful program.) Unsurprisingly, we feel that that target is simply too low; we will strive for double that: 20 new FTEs after the second year of the program.

Depth & Width

This program widens the scope of Spaceworks, and creates depth with newly introduced technical content. In a practical sense, it combines a trade school with a business school in a format measured in weeks! Results are both quick and substantive.

Funding

LPGR

Sources & Uses of Funds: Rich Uncle Model

In reference to a conversation in which the City of Tacoma (partially) funds Spaceworks, and Spaceworks contracts to LPGR: A recent well intended critique of our business plan called it the Rich Uncle Model. Initially, there was no strong rebuttal

Later, LPGR came to embrace that notion! And why not? The patronage model has worked since the Medicis funded Leonardo; the model holds today: BezosBlue Origin, MuskSpaceX, AllenXPrize, BransonVirgin Galactic, JainOdyssey Moon, BrinPlanetary Resources. Each of these revolutionary space companies are personally financed by a billionaire patron. Candidly, it is the only model that is working within this industry. So why not acknowledge that, though the orchestration of Spaceworks, the City of Tacoma is a patron (albeit not on the billion dollar scale) of a grand adventure? This grand adventure has immediate payoffs - jobs, economic stimulus - that the rich uncle wants; those payoffs facilitate what LPGR wants - catalytic and leveragable revenues, and catalytic and leveragable technology development. Spaceworks is our first client; they will be the initial spark, which will fan into a larger flame. Spaceworks will not be the only client of LPGR.

Contract funds will be used for staff, contractors, rent, insurance, utilities, strategic reserve, and marketing.

Spaceworks / Chamber

Operational Oversight

An essential component of this agreement, is that Spaceworks due to its track record of success be the operational partner in this training program. Beyond having access to the Creative Enterprise course curriculum and methodology, Spaceworks must be an engaged, informed, and active client of LPGR. To that end, as it relates to this specific contract, Spaceworks will have oversight into the workings of LPGR. Spaceworks will regularly review the budget, be provided with timely reports, and advise on strategic and tactical decisions facing LPGRs execution of this contract.

This training agreement extends and expands Spaceworks reach, capabilities, and community-centric results. In short, there will be greater city-wide vitality because Spaceworks: Technical exists.

Overhead Requirements

LPGR is asking Spaceworks for $135,000, annually, for a two year contract. Due to the above referenced Operational Oversite, it is assumed that Spaceworks will increase that initial ask, such that they cover their own needs. This additional ask is not the purview of LPGR, and is, necessarily, between the City and Spaceworks.

Goals Underserved Communities

In the context of this new program, there are three kinds of underserved communities: the expected answer of economically disadvantaged, and culturally disconnected. We will certainly focus our marketing to reach these people. But also there is, mixed in this melting pot of a class, the educated, credentialed and capitalized entrepreneur that is seeking access to industrial grade equipment that simply isnt available in the local market. This program will be for the maker who has outgrown their hobby in the garage; for the young student with an idea for a clever widget; for the person working two dead-end jobs and has a vision of a better future; for the retired, experienced, professional who has just one more thing theyd like to try; and it will be for the creator that wants to incorporate a new, tangible, aspect into their art.

To achieve this, the training will take place in a convenient location, central to the above mentioned communities. Hilltop is ideal; the brewery and dome districts would be helpful, or near UPS would be fine.

Because this is a nascent program that is piggy-backing off the established Spaceworks brand, marketing will be a mixed effort. First, it will need reach that Spaceworks doesnt currently have, because LPGR is targeting businesses that Spaceworks doesnt currently cater to. Second, the cache and the prior results of Spaceworks should open doors that a typical start-up educational program might not have access to. Third, the natural marketing channel is to current Spaceworks alumni that need the advanced-level equipment training (or refresher business training) that LPGR will provide. Fourth, outreach conversations with the schools in the area: SAMI/SOTA/IDEA, Summit, Bellarmine, Wrights, Bates, TCC, UWT, UPS, PLU, and in particular all of the regional high school vocational programs. Fifth, because this is a regionally-unique program, some marketing is warranted regionally: from Everett, through Seattle, Bellevue, Redmond, Renton, Kent, and as far south as Joint Base Lewis McCord and Olympia.

Goals From Vacancy to Vitality

Spaceworks works at the goals of its tagline. They are successful. Which is why LPGR wants to partner with them. However, beyond storefronts, there are other spaces to fill: warehouses, factories, manufacturing garages, and commercial office space. Spaceworks: Technical would like to help Spaceworks fill these other locations with advanced technology, specialty development, prototype/one-of-a-kind systems, and mass production of light manufacturing. Specialty service companies that provide resources to these companies would be included. This is a (mostly) untapped niche of the current Spaceworks program.

Goals Capacity Building

All of the efforts of this proposed training program, Spaceworks: Tacoma, will be focused on (further) raising the bar in Tacoma. The criteria LPGR will be judged against is our ability to develop new, fulltime jobs, increase the business and technical skills of advanced technology entrepreneurs, increase aggregate total revenues from incubated client companies, increase exports from these companies beyond the borders of Pierce county, the State, and the country; and to file patents and other intellectual property.
